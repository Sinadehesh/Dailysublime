Featured Artist

Vincent van Gogh
The Starry Night · 1889

Van Gogh painted this masterpiece from the east-facing window of his asylum room at Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, capturing not what he saw, but what he felt, a swirling cosmos of emotion rendered in oil and canvas.

Sunflowers, 1888

Why It Matters

Van Gogh's work reminds us that art is not about perfect representation, it's about authentic expression. His visible brushstrokes and bold colors broke from academic tradition, paving the way for Expressionism and modern art. Every swirl carries emotion; every color choice tells a story of inner experience.

✦ Five Key Observations

I
The cypress tree dominates the left side, connecting earth to sky like a dark flame.
II
Spiraling brushstrokes create movement throughout the sky, suggesting cosmic energy.
III
The village below remains peaceful and grounded, contrasting with the turbulent heavens.
